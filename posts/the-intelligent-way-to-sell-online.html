The Intelligent Way to Sell Online

By Gbenga Oso on December 13, 2020

The intelligent man knows that sometimes, shortcuts don’t work. So when the time comes, he digs his heels in and persists. – Niklas Göke

You have spent months picking the right business for your small enterprise. You are now ready to launch. Up till now, your marketing has been by word-of-mouth. To anyone who will listen, you tell your tale and ask for a sale. But you know you need to reach beyond confines of your neighbourhood to really succeed. Having only a small budget you think of going online. The internet offers much lower barriers to finding and converting potential customers than traditional print and broadcast media.

An important question at this point for many entrepreneurs is whether to invest in one’s own website or connect and convert on someone else’s. The lure to choose the shortcut is great with social networking services like Facebook and Instagram being popular choices. It costs more to build a website than to sign-up for a Facebook account. It certainly helps that there are over 20 million Nigerians on Facebook.

Why you Need a Website

The case for investing in a website is stronger than you may think. The trick is to think about your return on investment (ROI) over time. If done right, your website becomes a marketing system that gives and keeps on giving in the form of an endless stream of referrals and repeat sales.

Your website is your real estate on the web, and like a physical building your equity grows with your site’s development and maintenance. You build your website block by block with content that your target audience cares about, while positioning yourself as the trustworthy, likeable expert with the right solution to their problem.

As you may have realised by now, this only works for entrepreneurs who strongly care about their customers. You can’t fake the commitment required to create and sustain the value that delights your customers.

Telling Your Story

You know your craft well. You know your target audience, their goals and their pains. You also have a quality solution that meets your audience’s needs. Now it is time to tell your story in a way that resonates with your audience.

Like many business owners, you may find this work daunting. The resources on this site will provide you with the insights and tools for easily creating content that attracts and converts. Click here to learn more.

How to use Social Media

Social media sites are where you go to spread the word about your offerings and attract prospects to your website. Unlike on your website, social media sites impose restrictions on the content and format of your posts. Twitter allows no more than 280 characters per Tweet. Facebook has monetisation rules that cover the originality of your business content. These restrictions are one more reason you should have a website that you control.

Business owners often complain about how hard it is to communicate their product and service offerings to their target audience on social media. Unclear communication results in mismatched expectations, delays in order fulfilment and increased operational costs. The simple solution to this is to describe your offerings in great detail on your website and link to this content from your social media posts.

What about online Marketplaces?

Do a search on Google for “how to sell online in Nigeria” and you will be presented with pages of results about selling in online marketplaces. Local online marketplaces like Jumia and Konga provide an outlet for entrepreneurs to sell their products and services online. They attract buyer traffic through extensive online and offline advertisements. Like social media networks, these sites impose content and usage restrictions. Listing on these sites does not guarantee sales performance. Some reasons for this are low seller visibility, weak user review systems, price competition and faulty marketplace processes.

Owning your own website gives you more control over this situation. You tell your story on your website and link that story to your products in the marketplace. Rather than rely on the marketplace to promote your products, you drive traffic to your product pages through your marketing activities on your site and on social media.

If you are building to last, there are no shortcuts. Investing in your own website is a requirement for converting prospects into customers, and customers into loyal customers.
